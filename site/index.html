What is Venmo?

Venmo is a mobile application that allows users to easily pay their friends and authorized retailers. With just a few taps, users can connect their bank information and start paying their friends back for food, taxis, drinks, and more. Venmo has become especially popular on college campuses where students use it to split bills and pay each other back. Controversially, Venmo by default reveals all transactions via their public API. However, users often don't notice, exposing their transactions to the general public.

For our project, we explored what types of insights one can glean from this public data by visualizing the transactions within our network of friends on Venmo.

What do people use venmo for?

Each Venmo transaction can be categorized according to its purpose: what is being paid for. We categorized more than 350 of the most commonly used words and emojis in transactions captions. Transactions themselves are categorized based on which keywords they contain.

As can be seen, many transactions can't be categorized. Venmo is used for so many reasons, that not all transactions can be captured by neat categories. However, significant numbers of transactions are used to pay for food, transportation (predominantly ridesharing apps like Uber and Lyft), and drinks.

Timeline of hangouts

Venmo transactions are a great window into group hangouts. If a bunch of friends share a pizza, they'll probably use Venmo to split the cost. If a house hosts a formal, they might give students the option of paying for their ticket with Venmo. In order to find these kinds of "hangouts", we sorted our data into groups of transactions made to the same user within three hours of each other. Explore different hangouts on the timeline below.

What do people spend on and when?

Venmo transactions vary over time. The most transactions are made during the school year when college students, one of Venmo's most active userbases, are together. More transactions are made during weekends too, likely due to friends paying each other for meals, rides, and drinks after going out together.
